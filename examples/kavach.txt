Kavach is a distributed SIL-4 Automatic Train Protection (ATP) system comprising stationary, onboard, telecom, and supervisory subsystems interconnected through safety-critical communication interfaces over partially untrusted transmission networks.

The system includes geographically distributed Stationary Kavach nodes interfacing with Electronic Interlocking systems, onboard train protection units, radio communication infrastructure, centralized monitoring systems, and adjacent station handover interfaces.

Kavach employs deterministic fail-safe communication principles, redundant communication paths, cryptographic session establishment, heartbeat supervision, and continuous train movement authority enforcement to ensure operational safety and collision avoidance.

The architecture contains multiple trust zones including enterprise IT, IDMZ, supervisory control, stationary safety control, interlocking field interfaces, telecom transport infrastructure, and mobile onboard safety domains.

Safety-critical interfaces utilize protected communication protocols including RaSTA, TLS, CRC validation, heartbeat supervision, and session integrity verification in compliance with SIL-4 railway safety requirements and EN 50159 safe communication principles.
